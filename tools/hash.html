Frequently asked questions

What is a hash used for?

A hash turns text into a fixed-length fingerprint used to verify integrity, compare values, or store references without exposing the original.

Which algorithms are supported?

The tool computes MD5, SHA-1, and SHA-256. SHA-256 uses the browser's secure Web Crypto API.

Is hashing the same as encryption?

No. A hash is one-way and cannot be reversed, whereas encryption is designed to be decrypted.

Is my input uploaded?

No. Hashing runs locally in your browser and your text never leaves your device.
