Frequently asked questions

Is there a limit to how much text I can encode?

Yes — roughly 210 characters for plain text/URLs. Longer text needs a bigger, denser QR code, and beyond that limit the tool will show an error.

Can I make a QR code for Wi-Fi login?

Yes. Use the format WIFI:T:WPA;S:YourNetworkName;P:YourPassword;; and most phone cameras will offer to join the network when scanned.

Does the QR code expire or stop working?

No. The QR code is a static image encoding your exact text — it works forever and isn't tied to any account or server.

Is my data uploaded anywhere?

No. The QR code is generated entirely in your browser using JavaScript — your text never leaves your device.

Will it scan correctly on any phone?

Yes. It follows the standard QR code specification, so any modern phone camera or QR scanner app can read it.
