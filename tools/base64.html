Frequently asked questions

      

        What is Base64 used for?
        
Base64 encodes binary or text data into ASCII so it can travel safely in text-only channels like emails, JSON, URLs, and data URLs.

      

      

        Is my text uploaded when I encode or decode?
        
No. Encoding and decoding happen locally in your browser; your text never leaves your device.

      

      

        What is the difference between encode and decode?
        
Encode converts plain text into a Base64 string; decode reverses it, turning a Base64 string back into the original text.

      

      

        Can I Base64 a large file?
        
For text it works fine, but for files use the Image to Base64 tool, which is built for binary data.

      

      

        Does this Base64 tool work with large text or files?
        
It handles typical text payloads smoothly in-browser. For very large files, use the dedicated Image to Base64 tool, which is optimised for that.
