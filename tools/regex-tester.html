Frequently asked questions

What regex flavor does this use?

This tool uses your browser's native JavaScript regular expression engine, the same one used in Node.js and modern web apps.

What do the flags mean?

g finds all matches instead of just the first, i ignores case, m makes ^ and $ match line starts/ends, s lets . match newlines, and u enables full unicode mode.

Why does my pattern show an error?

The error message shows exactly what JavaScript's regex engine rejected — usually an unescaped special character or unbalanced brackets/parentheses.

Can I see capture groups from my matches?

Yes. Each match in the list below the highlight shows its full match text plus any capture groups, in order.

Is my text or pattern sent anywhere?

No. Matching runs entirely in your browser using JavaScript's built-in RegExp — nothing is uploaded or stored.
